The American Civil War (1861-1865)

Causes of the Civil War:
- Slavery was the central issue. Southern states relied on enslaved labor for their plantation economy, especially cotton.
- States' rights: Southern states believed they had the right to govern themselves, including the right to maintain slavery.
- Economic differences: The North was industrialized while the South was agrarian.
- The election of Abraham Lincoln in 1860 was the final trigger. He opposed the expansion of slavery into new territories.
- Seven Southern states seceded before Lincoln took office, forming the Confederate States of America.

Key Figures:
- Abraham Lincoln: 16th President of the United States. Issued the Emancipation Proclamation in 1863.
- Jefferson Davis: President of the Confederate States of America.
- Ulysses S. Grant: Union general who led the North to victory. Later became the 18th President.
- Robert E. Lee: Confederate general, considered one of the finest military commanders. Surrendered at Appomattox Court House.
- Frederick Douglass: Escaped slave and abolitionist who advocated for Black soldiers to join the Union Army.

Major Battles:
- Fort Sumter (April 1861): First battle of the war. Confederate forces fired on the Union fort in South Carolina.
- Battle of Gettysburg (July 1863): Turning point of the war. Union victory in Pennsylvania. Over 50,000 casualties.
- Battle of Antietam (September 1862): Bloodiest single day in American history with about 23,000 casualties.
- Siege of Vicksburg (1863): Union gained control of the Mississippi River, splitting the Confederacy.
- Sherman's March to the Sea (1864): Union General Sherman marched from Atlanta to Savannah, destroying Confederate infrastructure.

The Emancipation Proclamation (January 1, 1863):
- Declared all enslaved people in Confederate states to be free.
- Did not free slaves in border states that remained loyal to the Union.
- Shifted the war's purpose to include the abolition of slavery.
- Allowed Black men to serve in the Union Army and Navy.

End of the War:
- Robert E. Lee surrendered to Ulysses S. Grant at Appomattox Court House on April 9, 1865.
- Approximately 620,000 soldiers died during the war.
- The 13th Amendment, ratified in December 1865, abolished slavery throughout the United States.
- Lincoln was assassinated by John Wilkes Booth on April 14, 1865, just days after Lee's surrender.
